What I do is pretty simple.
In retrospect.

1. I question everything.

My first job is to understand your business as a total system and find out what is standing in the way of growth. The inconsistencies. The conflicting motivations. The misaligned incentives. The leaky buckets. The data gaps. The outdated tech. The bloated org structure. The brand that is of touch with modern culture. Whatever it is that is slowing you down, we will find it.

2. I kick everything.

I get stuck in and hands on. Because it's easy to be a consultant who never has to face reality and feel how it punches your plans in the mouth. I actually get my hands dirty and execute things (marketing, operations, product, MVP development), using and adjusting my own solutions, until real measurable results start showing up consistently.

3. I leave.

My goal is to make myself redundant. I dont like sticking around forever. Once Ive helped you remove the barriers to growth and set up a system that will keep you moving - my job is done. I pack it up and move on. If I did my job well, youll call me again when you are ready to give your growth path the next kick.
